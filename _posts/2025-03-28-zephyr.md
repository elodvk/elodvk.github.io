# Review of the Zephyr Pro Lab by Hack the Box: A Comprehensive Analysis

## The Zephyr Lab Story: A Simple Yet Engaging Scenario

The **Zephyr Pro Lab** presents a relatively straightforward scenario. **Zephyr Server Management** has been contracted by a company called **Painters** to maintain its infrastructure as the organization expands. As part of their security protocols, Painters undergo quarterly penetration tests. You are hired to identify vulnerabilities within their internal network and to evaluate the risk of potential compromises to both the Painters and Zephyr networks. Your primary objective is to explore the corporate environment, move across trust boundaries, and ultimately compromise both networks.

In terms of realism, the story does not provide an immersive, real-world corporate experience. It serves more as a framework for users to apply their skills in **Active Directory (AD)** environments. Like most Pro Labs, the focus is on identifying and exploiting misconfigurations, and the narrative doesn’t demand extensive engagement with business operations or organizational structure.

## Technical Skills Required: A Beginner-Friendly AD Lab

Zephyr is, at its core, an **Active Directory lab**. There are no complex web applications or advanced security features—just basic AD concepts and configurations. This makes it an ideal environment for individuals new to AD exploitation, and certainly easier than other Pro Labs like **Offshore**. Hack the Box markets Zephyr as “a great introductory lab for Active Directory,” and I believe this is an accurate description.

The lab focuses on enumerating the network, identifying misconfigurations, and exploiting common vulnerabilities. While the platform does not provide direct training material, users with a strong foundation in AD concepts will find the lab accessible. It took me around a week to complete the lab, and afterward, I revisited it to refine my notes and try out new attack techniques.

That being said, the lab could benefit from further challenges. As it stands, the **17 flags** are relatively easy to find, and the exploitation path is quite linear. While this may be appealing for beginners, it leaves little room for more experienced users to explore deeper, hidden objectives.

### Suggestions for Improvement

While the **Zephyr Pro Lab** serves as a solid introduction to AD exploitation, there is room for enhancement. Adding additional internal services or more complex enterprise simulations (similar to those found in the **Offshore** lab) could provide a more realistic and engaging experience. More difficult, non-linear paths and hidden flags would also offer greater depth, appealing to more advanced users seeking additional challenges.

## Lab Support and Performance

Zephyr offers **daily lab reverts**, which means persistence is not a significant issue. However, users will need to set up pivoting configurations each time, which can be a bit tedious but is common in similar environments. The lab’s performance is generally good, though I encountered minor issues with specific servers, which were usually resolved quickly.

While there is no direct technical support available for the lab, the Hack the Box Discord community is an excellent resource. The community is active, and members are often willing to offer guidance and share insights. This collaborative environment can help you overcome challenges when you hit a roadblock.

## Final Thoughts

Overall, I would highly recommend the **Zephyr Pro Lab**, particularly for users looking for a beginner-friendly AD lab that is not too overwhelming. Its price point—at just **$45 per month** for access to all Pro Labs—is quite reasonable, especially given the quality of the content. While the lab is not as complex or immersive as some other Pro Labs, it provides an excellent opportunity to hone fundamental AD exploitation skills.

Hack the Box has done a great job introducing new content to their platform, and I look forward to seeing what future Pro Labs will bring. With more advanced and business-specific labs on the horizon, Hack the Box is clearly evolving to meet the growing demands of the cybersecurity training community.
